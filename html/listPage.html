Great White Shark

The Great White Shark is an incredible animal. Females tend to grow to over 20 feet and weigh more than a ton. They are one of the largest ocean predators. Movies, likes Jaws, created mass hysteria and fear regarding the shark. This has caused them to decline in numbers over the years due to fishing. Recent activies, namely shark week, have given the shark a new found appreciation. New conservation efforts around the world are protecting them from going extinct.

Hammerhead Shark

Although there heads look funky, these sharks are another apex predator. Their hammer head, or cephalofoil, allows them to find prey easier. Their eyes are further away giving them an increased visual range. In addition, the wide head allows their receptors to locate fish easier. These massive sharks are known to congregate into massive groups of over a thousand!

Tiger Shark

Tiger sharks are known for eating anything that comes in its way. Studies have shown them to eat sharks, sea turtles, and even tires! They are also one of the most dangerous sharks to encounter in the wild. Responsible for many fatal shark bites. Tiger sharks also frequent coastal water where people tend to swim, further increasing the possiblility of an encounter.

Bull Shark

While the smallest of the sharks on this list, they make up for it with their aggresion. These sharks are known for their aggresive behavior and headputting their prey before biting it. They are one of the few sharks capable swimming in rivers. The murky water in rivers prevents them from accurately seeing what type of prey they are pursuing. This can cause humans to be falsey attacked.
